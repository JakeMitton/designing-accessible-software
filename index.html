About this resource

This site accompanies a video lecture on accessibility in software design, produced as part of a senior research project at Vancouver Island University, supervised by Dr. Sarah Carruthers.

The video covers what accessibility means for software developers, the standards that define it, the tools that help you build accessibly, and the practices that go beyond tools. Whether you are here after watching the video or exploring on your own, the resources below are a good place to start.

Further Reading

  • WCAG 2.1 Quick Reference (opens in new tab) Filter and browse every WCAG 2.1 success criterion by principle and conformance level.
  • MDN Accessibility Documentation (opens in new tab) Mozilla's comprehensive guide to building accessible web content, including HTML semantics and ARIA.
  • MDN ARIA Reference (opens in new tab) Complete reference for ARIA roles, properties, and states with usage guidance and examples.
  • WebAIM Contrast Checker (opens in new tab) Check the contrast ratio between any two colours against WCAG AA and AAA requirements.
  • WHO Disability Fact Sheet (opens in new tab) World Health Organization statistics and information on disability and health worldwide.
  • The Curb Cut Effect — PolicyLink (opens in new tab) Angela Glover Blackwell's explanation of how designing for disability benefits everyone.
